About Me

Software Engineer & Adjunct

I was born and raised in Nigeria. I left at the age of 17 to pursure a college education at Eastern Mediterannean University in Famagusta, North Cyprus. After 4 years I graduated with a Bachelor's degree in Software Engineering with high honors. Upon graduation, I decided to further my education by going to graduate school. I moved to the US to attend The University of North Texas where I eventually graduated with a Master's and Ph.D. in Computer Science.

Since graduating I have several roles such as an R&D Software Engineer at Microsoft on the Azure Data Org, and an Adjunct Lecturer at the University of Texas at Austin. My areas of expertise include Software Engineering, Human Computer Interaction, and Accessibility.

I'm passionate about the following creating easy to use and accessible technology, increasing the participation of marginalized communities in computing, mentoring young people coming up in the tech industry, teaching and impacting knowledge in the next generation, public speaking and giving back to the community.

In my free time I like to listen to podcasts and audio books, work out, spend time with friends and family, learn new languages, solve word puzzles, try new restaurants, travel to new places, shop and do DIY projects in my home.

People will describe me as kind, empathetic, bold, smart, confident, talented, patient, resillient etc.
